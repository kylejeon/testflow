### 📌 POST T+7 — Apr 17 (금) 22:00 KST / Kyle / EN — Thought leadership (Testably 가볍게만)

```
A week into launching a side project, here's a thought I keep coming back to:

QA tools have been stuck in the past for too long.

Most test management software was designed in an era when:
→ Releases happened every 6 months
→ QA teams were separate from dev teams
→ Documentation was the goal, not the byproduct
→ Compliance reporting was the main use case

But software development has fundamentally changed:
✅ Releases happen weekly (or daily)
✅ QA is embedded in dev teams
✅ Speed and clarity matter more than documentation volume
✅ Real-time visibility beats end-of-quarter reports

Yet most QA tools still feel like they were built for 2010.

This is the gap I'm trying to close with Testably. Not by adding more features, but by stripping away everything that doesn't serve modern QA workflows.

I don't think we'll get it 100% right. But I think the direction matters.

What would YOU change about your current QA tool if you could?
```
